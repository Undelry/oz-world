## Preamble

OZ Coin (OZC) is not just another cryptocurrency. It is the foundational currency of OZ World — an AI-powered virtual metaverse where a child singing a song can earn money from people around the globe.

OZC is designed with a long-term vision spanning decades: to become a global reserve currency accessible to all 8 billion people on Earth, without regard to age, nationality, or wealth.

This document is the constitution governing OZC's development, governance, and future. It is a living contract between the founder, the community, and all future holders.

## Article 1: Core Principles

### 1.1 Accessibility First
OZC shall remain accessible to anyone, anywhere, without age restrictions, KYC requirements, or geographic limitations. A 5-year-old singing a song in Tokyo must be able to receive OZC from an admirer in New York.

### 1.2 Security by Design
While keeping barriers low, security shall never be compromised. All transactions shall be transparent, verifiable, and tamper-proof via the Solana blockchain.

### 1.3 Long-term over Short-term
OZC is built for the next 100 years, not the next 100 days. All decisions shall prioritize long-term sustainability over short-term profit.

### 1.4 Transparency
All actions affecting supply, distribution, or governance shall be publicly announced and recorded on-chain.

## Article 2: Token Economics

### 2.1 Initial Supply

### 2.2 Mint Authority Policy

The mint authority is **retained by the founder** in Phase 0 (current phase). This is a conscious decision, not an oversight.

**Why retention, not renouncement?**

Permanently renouncing mint authority creates a fixed supply, which leads to deflation as user base grows. History has shown (1930s Gold Standard) that deflationary currencies cannot function as reserve currencies. Bitcoin's fixed supply of 21 million coins is why it remains a "store of value" rather than evolving into a global reserve currency.

OZC's vision requires monetary flexibility to serve billions of users across decades. Therefore, mint authority must be retained — but with strict, transparent rules.

### 2.3 Mint Authority Rules (Phase 0)

The founder commits to the following inviolable rules:

1. **Annual Inflation Cap**: Total supply shall not increase by more than 2% per calendar year.
2. **Public Announcement**: Every minting action shall be announced on GitHub and X (Twitter) at least 7 days in advance.
3. **Purpose Restriction**: Mints shall only be executed for:
   - Liquidity pool provisioning
   - Community rewards
   - Emergency security responses
   - Documented partnerships
4. **Emergency Stop**: Community may demand a halt via public petition; founder commits to respect consensus.
5. **Full On-chain Logging**: All mints are publicly visible on Solscan and recorded in the project repository.

## Article 3: Phased Governance Evolution

### Phase 0 — Seed (Current)
- **Mint authority**: Founder only
- **Update authority**: Founder only
- **Governance**: Founder discretion
- **Trust model**: Personal reputation + public commitments
- **Duration**: Until multi-sig setup (estimated Q3 2026)

### Phase 1 — Multi-sig (Target: Q3 2026)
- **Mint authority**: 2-of-3 multi-sig (Founder + 2 trusted contributors)
- **Update authority**: Multi-sig
- **Governance**: Weighted voting by OZC holders
- **Trust model**: Distributed responsibility

### Phase 2 — Smart Contract (Target: 2027)
- **Mint authority**: Program-Derived Address (PDA)
- **Rules**: Hard-coded inflation rate (1-3% yearly)
- **Governance**: OZC holder DAO
- **Trust model**: Code is law

### Phase 3 — Algorithmic (Target: 2030+)
- **Mint authority**: Fully algorithmic
- **Rules**: Inflation pegged to user growth + economic indicators
- **Governance**: Decentralized, gas-free voting
- **Trust model**: Pure economic math

### Phase 4 — Global Infrastructure (Target: 2035+)
- **Integration with real-world payments**
- **Bridges to other chains**
- **Partnership with regulators**
- **Recognition as a payment instrument**

### Phase 5 — Reserve Currency (Target: 2050+)
- **Held as reserve by institutions**
- **Used in international trade**
- **Global accessibility achieved**

## Article 7: Philosophy

### 7.1 Why OZC Must Not Fail

Over 80% of Earth's population does not have access to sophisticated financial tools. Children cannot open bank accounts. Creators in developing nations cannot receive international payments. Artists are exploited by platforms taking 30% cuts.

OZC exists to change this.

Every decision about OZC must pass this test:
> "Does this help a 10-year-old in Tokyo, a farmer in Kenya, a coder in Jakarta, and a grandmother in São Paulo to participate in the global creator economy?"

If the answer is yes, we proceed. If not, we reconsider.

### 7.2 We Are Not Here to Get Rich

The founder is 16 years old. He did not create OZC to make himself wealthy. He created it because he saw a world where creators are undervalued and children are excluded from the creator economy.

If OZC succeeds, the founder's personal wealth is a byproduct, not a goal. The goal is a world where a child can sing a song and receive payment from people who heard it — no bank account, no KYC, no barriers.

### 7.3 We Are Not Alone

Bitcoin proved digital money is possible.
Ethereum proved programmable money is possible.
OZC will prove accessible money is possible.

We stand on the shoulders of giants, but we aim higher.
